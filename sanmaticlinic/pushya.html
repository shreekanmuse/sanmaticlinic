Sacred Pediatric Wellness

Suvarna Prashana Sanskara

A Traditional Ayurvedic Wellness Practice for Children

Nurturing Children's Health with Purified Gold & Sacred Herbs

Suvarna Prashana is an age-old Ayurvedic practice in which a specially prepared formulation containing purified gold (Suvarna Bhasma), ghee, honey (where appropriate), and selected herbs is administered under the guidance of a qualified Ayurvedic physician. It has traditionally been associated with supporting children's overall health, growth, and development.

Pushya Nakshatra Special

Pushya Nakshatra is considered an auspicious day in Ayurvedic tradition for administering Suvarna Prashana. It occurs approximately once every 27 days, and many families choose this occasion to continue their child's wellness routine.

Traditional Newborn Care

Golden Touch Ceremony

Some families also observe a traditional Ayurvedic newborn ritual in which a purified gold stick is gently rubbed to prepare a tiny quantity for ceremonial administration under the guidance of an experienced Ayurvedic physician. This practice forms part of traditional Ayurvedic newborn care and should only be performed by trained professionals using appropriate methods.
